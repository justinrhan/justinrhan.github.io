Palembang Canopy
Rattan, abaca, black walnut, plywood, pigment dye, machine-knit and hand-woven textile, hardware
68.5"x33.5"x3"

I locate many of the contours of this year's work in memories of a trip to Vietnam and Indonesia in 2023, where many a provisionally assembled form caught my eye. A pair of extinguished roadside wood stoves, devices giving new life to cylindrical tins, their garish print.

At one corner an umbrella cast in a hard plastic, both utility pole and rain shelter for Saigon traffic police.

I was struck more than anything by janur kuning, ornately woven and plaited palm leaves strung along a bamboo armature. Installed at domestic thresholds, they mark weddings and other momentous occasions. In Yogyakarta and Bali, I felt these objects fold me into the rhythms of lives running in parallel (and oblivious) to the tourist's, made so aware of their function as repositories for hope, beckoned fortunes, good will.
